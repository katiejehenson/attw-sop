Trip Statuses

Action Required ▼

Your current status list has grown too long and includes statuses that overlap or don't serve a clear purpose. Here's a recommended streamlined flow — and some questions for you about a few of them.

✅ Recommended Status Flow

🌱 New Lead → 🗺 Planning → 📄 Proposal Sent → ✅ Booked → 🗓 Pre-Travel → ✈️ Traveling → 🏡 Welcome Home
👻 Ghosted ✕ Cancelled 📦 Archived
ℹ️
Pre-Travel triggers automatically at 30 days out from the trip start date. This is when the pre-travel document wrap-up task fires and the final detail emails go out.

📝 Notes on Specific Statuses

Booked

This means the deposit has been paid and the trip is secured — but there may still be elements to plan (excursions, transfers, hotels, etc.). Booked ≠ fully planned.

Post-Travel → Removed

Post-Travel and Welcome Home have been merged into a single Welcome Home status. No need for two statuses at this stage.

Ghosted

Keep this as a tag rather than a status if you want to be able to filter — or use it as a status to clearly mark leads that went quiet. Either works.

Intake → Removed

Intake and Sent Planning don't map to a clear stage in the client journey. New Lead covers everything from first contact through your initial call.

❓ Questions for You, Christine

  • 💬Groups, Prospect Trips, Dream Ideas — what was your intention for these? Are they types of trips, pipeline categories, or something else? Knowing the purpose helps determine whether these should be statuses, tags, or trip types.
  • 💬Traveled vs. Stored — what does "Stored" mean to you? Is this different from Archived? If "Traveled" = trips that are complete, this maps to Welcome Home. "Stored" may simply be Archived.
  • 💬Archived vs. Cancelled — do you need both, or would a single "Inactive" category cover all trips that are no longer active? Simpler is usually better here.

Library

Build Out ▼

You currently have only 25 activity items in your library. This is an area with a big opportunity to save time — but the goal isn't to build from scratch. Start with what you already know.

💡
Best approach: Go through your existing active and recently traveled trips and pull the hotels, excursions, and transfers you book repeatedly. Save those to the library first. Building from real trips is faster and more useful than creating items speculatively.

Where to Start

1

Review active & recently traveled trips

Look at trips currently in Traveling, Pre-Travel, or Welcome Home status. Pull any hotels, transfers, or experiences you plan to book again.

2

Save frequently used hotels first

Hotels you book for Disney, cruises, or FIT trips should be saved to the library with your standard descriptions already personalized in your voice.

3

Build itinerary templates from real trips

Once you have library items saved, you can assemble them into reusable itinerary templates by destination — so new trips of the same type can be built in minutes, not hours.

4

Add activities, excursions & transfers

Shore excursions, airport transfers, and common day-trip options can all be library items. Add as you encounter them, not all at once.

Forms

In Progress ▼

Forms are where your client process begins. The initial trip inquiry form is your entry point into Tern — here's how it's designed to work.

How the Trip Inquiry Form Works

1

Client submits the Trip Inquiry Form

The form captures limited contact info and trip details — just enough to get started. It does not need to be exhaustive at this stage.

2

Tern automatically creates the contact and trip

When the form is submitted, Tern creates both a contact record and a new trip, labeled as New Lead. This is why New Lead is the only status you need at intake.

3

Save the form link as an email template in your email client

Keep a standard response email ready in Outlook or Gmail that includes the form link. When anyone asks about a trip, send that email — one click, and the process is started.

4

Discovery call → Agency Agreement

After the form is submitted, you have a conversation with the client. From there, the Agency Client Agreement email template already exists in Tern — send that to move forward.

All four All Trips workflows are now complete. Trip-specific workflows layer on top. Here's what each workflow covers.

01 — All Trips: Inbound Leads

Trigger: Trip Status → Inbound Lead

3 tasks + 1 email. The trigger has been updated from "Trip Created" to trip status = Inbound Lead — this gives you more control over when the workflow fires, so it's no longer tied to who or how the trip was created.

💡
With the status-based trigger, the Inbound Leads workflow only fires when a trip is set to New Lead status — not automatically on trip creation. This means you control the timing and the email always goes out at the right moment.

02 — All Trips: Planning

Trigger: Trip Status → Planning

Trip-agnostic planning steps that apply to every trip type. Includes: contact preferred supplier, travel insurance initial touchpoint, and Tern account setup reminder. These steps fire for FIT, cruise, Disney, and any other trip type — no duplication needed in trip-specific workflows.

03 — All Trips: Payments

Trigger: Trip Status → Booked

Fires immediately when a trip is marked Booked. Handles the complete payment lifecycle: deposit confirmation, 10-day reminder, 1-day reminder, 1-day-past-due notice, and final payment emails. Because this is centralized here, trip-specific workflows do not need their own payment reminder emails — it's already covered.
